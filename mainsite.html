TF2: HOW TO GIT GUD

The Scout

1. Master the Double Jump: Use the Scout's quick double jump to reach unexpected places, dodge enemy fire, and access tricky routes.

2. Use the Pistol Effectively: The Scout's pistol is a powerful secondary weapon. Practice quick, accurate shots to finish off enemies or disable key targets.

3. Know Your Map: Familiarize yourself with map layouts, shortcuts, and high ground spots to keep your enemies on their toes.

4. Stay Moving and Use Hit-and-Run Tactics: The Scout excels at hit-and-run gameplay. Keep moving to avoid being an easy target and pick off enemies quickly.

5. Focus on Flanking: Use speed to flank enemies and attack from unexpected angles, disrupting their formations.

6. Use the Sandman or Boston Basher (if allowed): For disruptive plays or defending objectives, these weapons can be very effective.

7. Play Smart with the Force-a-Nature: Its knockback can push enemies off edges or disrupt chokes, but be mindful of its recoil.

8. Prioritize Objectives: The Scout can be crucial for capturing points or pushing the cart due to his speed and agility.

9. Communicate with Your Team: Call out enemy positions and coordinate attacks for maximum impact.

10. Use the Scattergun for Close Combat: Get close to enemies for maximum damage, especially in chaotic skirmishes.

The Soldier

1. Master Rocket Jumping: Use your rocket launcher to propel yourself to high ground, cross gaps, or surprise enemies. Practice timing and positioning for safe and effective jumps.

2. Aim for Critical Hits: Aim for headshots to maximize damage. The Soldier's rocket launcher can deal critical hits when aimed precisely.

3. Use Cover and Terrain to Your Advantage: Use walls, corners, and high ground to protect yourself while firing. Rocket jumps can also help you reach advantageous positions.

4. Switch Between Primary and Secondary Weapons Wisely: Use the shotgun for close encounters and the Equalizer or other secondary weapons for specific situations.

5. Keep Moving: Constant movement makes it harder for enemies to hit you. Strafe and jump unpredictably.

6. Use the Concheror for Unexpected Pushes: When timed right, the Concheror can give you a speed boost and extra damage, useful for breaking enemy lines or pushing the cart.

7. Target High-Value Enemies: Focus on snipers, Medics, and Engineers to weaken enemy defenses and support your team.

8. Practice Aim and Leading Shots: Rocket travel time is a factor; lead enemy movement to land hits more consistently.

9. Communicate with Your Team: Call out enemy positions, especially snipers or Engineers that need to be taken out.

10. Use the Battlefield to Your Advantage: Take advantage of choke points, doorways, and narrow corridors to maximize rocket hits and splash damage.

The Pyro

1. Master Close-Range Combat: The Pyro excels at close quarters. Use your flamethrower to surprise enemies and push them back.

2. Use Airblast Effectively: The airblast can reflect projectiles, extinguish burning teammates, and push enemies away. Practice timing it to deflect rockets, grenades, or enemy projectiles.

3. Master Area Denial: Use your flamethrower to consistently pressure enemies around corners or behind cover. Aim at their feet or walls to control space.

4. Practice Flare Gun Combos: Combine flamethrower burns with flare gun shots for devastating damage combos on enemies.

5. Use Ambush Tactics: Pyro's backburner creates bonus damage when attacking from behind, making ambushes very effective.

6. Clear Chokepoints: Pyro can quickly clear enemy camps or choke points by spamming fire and using airblast strategically.

7. Use Secondary Weapons: Use the shotgun or other secondary weapons for finishing off fleeing enemies or when you need more range.

8. Stay Mobile: Keep moving to avoid being an easy target. Flank enemies from unexpected angles to catch them off guard.

9. Extinguish Burning Teammates: Always watch your team and extinguish teammates on fire to keep them alive longer.

10. Build Map Awareness: Know the map layout to find the best spots for ambushes, escape routes, and flanking.

The Demoman

1. Master Grenade Arcing: Learn to arc your grenades properly - they bounce and roll, so practice predicting where enemies will be when they explode.

2. Use Stickybomb Traps Strategically: Place sticky bombs around corners, on ceilings, or near health packs to catch enemies off guard when they least expect it.

3. Learn Sticky Jumping: Master sticky jumping to reach high ground, escape danger, or surprise enemies from unexpected angles - just watch your health!

4. Control Chokepoints: Use your explosive area damage to deny enemies access through doorways, corridors, and narrow passages.

5. Time Your Detonations: Don't detonate stickies immediately - let enemies walk into your traps or use the delay to your tactical advantage.

6. Protect Your Team's Flank: Set up sticky traps on alternate routes to prevent enemy flankers from sneaking up on your team.

7. Use the Grenade Launcher for Direct Combat: Practice direct hits with grenades for maximum damage - they do more damage on direct impact than after bouncing.

8. Reload Constantly: Both your primary and secondary weapons have long reload times, so reload whenever you have a safe moment.

9. Work with Your Team: Coordinate with Engineers to protect their buildings, and stay near Medics who can keep you healed through self-damage.

10. Learn Rollout Timing: On competitive maps, learn the sticky jump rollouts to get to mid-points quickly and gain positional advantages.

The Heavy

1. Pre-spin Your Minigun: Start spinning up your minigun before rounding corners or when you expect enemies - the spin-up time can be deadly if caught off guard.

2. Use Cover Effectively: You're a big, slow target, so always position yourself near walls, corners, or objects you can duck behind to avoid damage.

3. Master the Sandvich: Your Sandvich can fully heal you and can be thrown to heal teammates - use it strategically and find safe spots to eat it.

4. Stay with Your Team: Heavy works best when supporting teammates, especially near Medics, Engineers, or Pyros who can watch your back.

5. Control Your Movement: You move very slowly while spun up, so learn when to spin down and reposition versus when to stay spun up and tank damage.

6. Watch Your Flanks: Spies, Scouts, and Soldiers love to attack you from behind or above - constantly check around you and listen for footsteps.

7. Use the Fists of Steel: When moving between positions, switch to Fists of Steel to reduce ranged damage and get to safety faster.

8. Deny Area Access: Your massive damage output can completely shut down chokepoints and doorways - use this to control map areas.

9. Communicate with Medics: Call for Medic when you need healing, and protect them at all costs - a Heavy-Medic combo is incredibly powerful.

10. Learn Damage Falloff: Get as close as possible to enemies for maximum minigun damage - your weapon becomes much weaker at long range.

The Engineer

1. Master Sentry Placement: Place your sentry in corners or areas with good cover where it can watch multiple angles but enemies can't easily peek and destroy it.

2. Always Keep Metal Stocked: Constantly collect metal from ammo packs, dispensers, and fallen weapons - you need it for building, upgrading, and repairs.

3. Upgrade in Safe Spots: Build your buildings in safe areas away from enemy fire, then use the Rescue Ranger or carry them to better positions once upgraded.

4. Use Your Dispenser Strategically: Place dispensers near your team's fighting positions to keep everyone healed and supplied with ammo - they're often more valuable than sentries.

5. Watch for Spies Constantly: Spies are your biggest threat - constantly spycheck with your wrench, watch for suspicious teammates, and listen for decloak sounds.

6. Learn the Rescue Ranger: This weapon lets you heal buildings from long range and grab them to safety when they're under attack.

7. Build Teleporter Networks: Teleporters can completely change the game flow - upgrade them to level 3 and place exit points in safe but useful locations.

8. Use the Wrangler for Defense: The Wrangler shield can make your sentry nearly indestructible and extends its range - perfect for stopping enemy pushes.

9. Rotate Your Buildings: Don't get attached to one spot - move your buildings as the battle flow changes and team needs shift.

10. Support Your Team: Drop dispensers near the front lines, use your shotgun in combat, and remember that helping your team is often better than hiding behind your sentry.

The Medic

1. Stay Behind Cover: Always position yourself behind walls, corners, or teammates - you're a high-priority target and need protection to stay alive.

2. Master the Crossbow: The Crusader's Crossbow heals teammates from long range and does more healing the farther they are - practice your aim with it.

3. Overheal Everyone: Buff teammates to 150% health when you have time - overhealed players are much more effective in combat and survive longer.

4. Use Uber Strategically: Don't waste your Ubercharge - coordinate with your team and use it to push through chokepoints or counter enemy Ubers.

5. Prioritize Healing Targets: Heavy and Soldier first for damage, then other combat classes - but always heal anyone critically injured regardless of class.

6. Surf Damage to Escape: When taking knockback damage, use it to surf away from enemies and create distance to survive longer.

7. Communicate Constantly: Call out enemy positions, Spy warnings, and coordinate Uber pushes - your team relies on your information and leadership.

8. Stay Mobile and Unpredictable: Keep moving, use different routes, and don't be predictable - Snipers and Spies will target you first.

9. Watch Your Uber Percentage: Keep track of enemy Medic Uber percentages and communicate advantage/disadvantage to your team for better timing.

10. Use Multiple Medi-guns: The Kritzkrieg for offense, stock Uber for defense, and Quick-Fix for keeping your team alive during intense fights.

The Sniper

1. Master Your Scope Timing: Learn to quickscope for 150 damage headshots - you don't always need to fully charge for maximum damage against lighter classes.

2. Change Positions Frequently: Never stay in the same spot too long - enemy Snipers, Spies, and Soldiers will hunt you down if you're predictable.

3. Prioritize High-Value Targets: Focus on enemy Medics, other Snipers, and Engineers first - eliminating these classes helps your team push forward effectively.

4. Use the SMG for Close Combat: When enemies get close, switch to your SMG and backpedal while firing - don't try to noscope everything at close range.

5. Learn Common Sightlines: Memorize popular Sniper spots and angles on each map so you can pre-aim and counter-snipe effectively.

6. Communicate Enemy Positions: Call out flanking Scouts, Spies, and other threats to help your team - your elevated position gives you great vision.

7. Use the Razorback Against Spies: If enemy Spies are targeting you constantly, equip the Razorback to force them to use their gun instead of backstabbing.

8. Body Shot When Necessary: Sometimes a 150 damage body shot is better than missing a headshot - especially against low-health enemies trying to escape.

9. Watch Your Flanks Constantly: Check behind you regularly and listen for footsteps - Scouts and Spies love to target isolated Snipers.

10. Use Jarate for Team Support: Throw Jarate at groups of enemies or on teammates who are on fire - the mini-crits help your entire team deal more damage.

The Spy

1. Master Your Disguises: Use enemy disguises that make sense for the situation - avoid slow classes like Heavy when you need to move quickly, and pick common classes like Soldier or Scout.

2. Learn Backstab Angles: Practice getting behind enemies smoothly - you need to be directly behind them for the backstab to register, so work on your positioning and timing.

3. Use Your Revolver Effectively: Don't rely only on backstabs - the revolver does good damage and can finish off weakened enemies from a safe distance.

4. Time Your Decloaks Carefully: Decloak behind cover where enemies can't hear you - the decloak sound is very loud and will give you away if you're not careful.

5. Target High-Priority Enemies: Focus on Medics, Engineers, Snipers, and other key targets that will help your team push forward when eliminated.

6. Use the Dead Ringer Strategically: Fake your death when taking damage, then use the speed boost and damage resistance to escape or reposition for another attack.

7. Sap Buildings in the Right Order: Sap the sentry first to stop it from firing, then quickly sap other buildings - or sap the dispenser first if the Engineer isn't nearby.

8. Act Like Your Disguise: Walk toward "enemy" positions, don't look directly at enemies, and occasionally shoot at "teammates" to sell your disguise convincingly.

9. Learn Common Engineer Spots: Memorize where Engineers typically build so you can find and eliminate their nests more efficiently.

10. Stay Patient and Pick Your Moments: Don't rush into groups of enemies - wait for isolated targets or distracted enemies during team fights.
